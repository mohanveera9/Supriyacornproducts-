Corn Grits

Corn grits are coarsely ground bits of dried corn, versatile in cooking and a staple in Southern cuisine. They range in texture from fine to coarse, offering a hearty, corn-flavored base for various dishes.

Uses:

  • Consumed as a side dish for breakfast.
  • Majorly used for extruded snacks.
  • Used as an ingredient in the brewing industry to help with fermentation and enhance the flavor.

Flaking Grits/Brewery Grits

Flaking grits are produced by steaming corn kernels and then flattening them between rollers. These grits create thin, uniform flakes that cook quickly while retaining much of the corn's natural flavor and nutritional content.

  • Creates thin, uniform flakes that cook quickly
  • Retains much of the corn's natural flavor and nutritional content
  • Popular choice for breakfast dishes and snacks
  • Applications: Bakery, Beverages, Confectionery, Convenience Food, Nutritional Supplements, Snacks/Cereals

Uses:

  • Commonly used in breakfast cereals and porridge, providing a hearty and nutritious option.

Corn Flour

Corn flour is a finely ground powder made from dried corn kernels. It has a smooth texture and is often used as a thickening agent in soups, sauces, and gravies, or as a base for baked goods like cornbread, muffins, and pancakes.

  • Retains the natural sweetness and flavor of corn
  • Popular ingredient in both savory and sweet recipes
  • Naturally gluten-free, suitable for those with gluten sensitivities or celiac disease
  • Provides essential nutrients like fiber, vitamins, and minerals

Corn Bran

Corn bran is the outer layer of the corn kernel, rich in dietary fiber, antioxidants, and essential nutrients such as vitamins B and E. During the milling process, corn bran is separated from the kernel and often added to food products to enhance their nutritional profile.

  • Commonly used in cereals, breads, muffins, and snack bars
  • Provides a substantial boost in fiber content, aiding in digestion and promoting satiety
  • Helps regulate blood sugar levels
  • Retains much of the corn's natural flavor, adding a subtle sweetness and a slightly nutty taste to recipes

Uses:

  • Valuable source of energy and protein for livestock feed, supporting growth and overall health. Commonly included in rations for poultry, swine, and cattle.
